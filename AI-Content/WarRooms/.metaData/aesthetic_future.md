# Aesthetic — Futuristic War (WW2 × Cyberpunk Fusion)

**Source of truth:** [`aesthetic.md`](./aesthetic.md). This file specializes the canon for the **dense cyberpunk-fused expression of Era III** ("Near-future cyber-WW2").
**Status:** Drafted 2026-05-12 from a ChatGPT prompt-bake to push Era III further. Optional aesthetic mode — reserve for milestone moments, same restraint guidance as base Era III.

---

## When to use this

Use when:
- The operation is AI-native — autonomous systems, agent orchestration, ML deployment, cybernetic-adjacent themes.
- A milestone moment deserves a more cinematic, denser treatment than base Era III.
- The base WW2 aesthetic feels too restrained for the subject matter.

Do NOT use as the default. Every poster in this mode dilutes its weight. Parallel guidance to `aesthetic.md` §5 Mode C (dark variant) — keep it rare.

For everything else, use `aesthetic.md` Era I or Era II.

---

## Core concept

Blend **retro-futurist cyberpunk dystopia** with **WWII American propaganda aesthetics**. A "World War AI" battlefield where 1940s wartime imagery collides with advanced cybernetic warfare.

**Thematic rule:** the visual era is still fundamentally **1940s wartime propaganda** — but the conflict is fought with AI systems, cybernetics, software deployments, autonomous warfare, and digital infrastructure. The tension between analog military grit and futuristic technology IS the identity.

The anachronism rule from `aesthetic.md` §9 still holds, with a cybernetic twist: a 1944 GI shouting into a fiber-optic field radio about a `QUERY TIMEOUT`. An officer with a HUD visor pointing at a holographic map showing `400 INVALID TENANT PLAN`. A trench engineer welding cybernetic armor to a soldier while debugging a `feature flag` on a CRT monitor.

## Setting / environment

- **Rain-soaked neo-noir megacity** transformed into a wartime command front.
- **Massive arcology bunkers** and **cyber-factories**.
- **Holographic battle maps** projected over wooden strategy tables.
- **CRT monitors** displaying system failures, GUIDs, deployment maps, JSON fragments, feature flags, tactical diagnostics.
- **Server-rack trenches** and **fortified data-centers** functioning as bunkers.
- **Vintage radios** connected to glowing fiber-optic cables.
- **AI faction banners** hanging beside wartime propaganda posters.
- **Surveillance drones** flying through artillery smoke.
- **Underground war rooms** where mech-knights and signal-corps hackers coordinate attacks.

Atmospherics: rain, smoke, sparks, code-rain, fog, burning neon signage. **Volumetric fog and rain are the default weather** in this mode.

## Figures

WW2-era soldiers, officers, resistance fighters, radio operators, trench engineers — all wearing **olive-drab military uniforms fused with cybernetic augmentations**:

- Glowing HUD visors (cyan, orange, or holographic-blue).
- Mech-assisted armor on shoulders, arms, or legs.
- Analog targeting systems (cross-hair monocles, brass-and-glass).
- Retro-futuristic gear — handheld terminals, side-arm scanners, fiber-optic radio sets.
- Cybernetic soldiers carrying briefcases labeled with software artifacts and deployment documents.

The faction canon in `aesthetic_faction_claude.md`, `aesthetic_faction_chatgpt.md`, and `aesthetic_faction_githubcopilot.md` still applies *when a faction appears in the scene* — same archetypes, denser environment. **Faction presence is not the default in Era III posters.** A typical Era III VDS / Indago Operation poster shows the team alone in its cyber-trench environment, no factions flanking. Include Claude / ChatGPT / Copilot only when the work item explicitly involves AI collaboration or cross-cutting AI themes — see [`prompt_ado_item_poster.md`](./prompt_ado_item_poster.md) §"Factions are additive, not default" for the rule.

In this mode, the *human* foreground figures get cybernetic augmentations too, matching the world.

## Palette

Era-I core + cyberpunk neon accents. **Neon is used sparingly against muted military tones** — the propaganda heritage stays dominant.

**Core (from `aesthetic.md` §3, unchanged):**
- **Parchment cream** `#E8D9B0`–`#F2E5C1` — aged-paper textures, type.
- **Burnt orange / ochre** `#C5602D`–`#E0813C` — accents, neon-glow warmth.
- **Olive drab / khaki** `#5A6342`–`#7B7B4A` — uniforms (cybernetically augmented).
- **Propaganda red** `#A8341F`–`#C53A22` — banners, alarm indicators.
- **Deep brown / chocolate** `#3E2A1C`–`#5C3A24` — shadows, foreground figures.
- **Black chrome** `#0F0F0F` — outlines, cybernetic armor.

**Cyberpunk additions:**
- **Electric cyan** `#3FB3D8` — HUD visors, holographic displays, fiber-optic cables.
- **Holographic blue** `#1F5FA8`–`#3FB3D8` — projected battle maps, drone overlays.
- **Steel navy** `#1E2A3A` — cyber-armor, server-rack shadows.
- **Neon glow** — sparingly, as set-dressing on burning signage. Never the dominant color.

**Critical balance rule:** the parchment-cream and aged-paper backbone MUST survive. If the image reads as "Cyberpunk 2077 fan art" instead of "WW2 propaganda with cyberpunk elements," the palette balance has tipped wrong — pull neon back, push grain and propaganda framing forward.

## Stylistic direction

- **High-contrast cinematic lighting** — chiaroscuro, dramatic single-source light, volumetric fog.
- **Painterly propaganda composition** — figure placement, sunburst rays, banner framing per `aesthetic.md` §7.
- **Distressed textures and aged print grain** — non-negotiable.
- **Volumetric fog and rain** — wet streets, smoke, atmospheric haze.
- **Retro-futuristic military iconography** — analog dials on cybernetic gear, brass-and-chrome.
- **Dense environmental storytelling** — every corner of the frame has a labeled artifact, CRT, banner, drone, or trench detail.
- **Stylized realism, not photorealism** — painterly illustration, not 3D render look.
- **WW2 propaganda poster framing mixed with cyberpunk holographics** — title bar, tagline banner, thin cream border survive even in dense scenes.

## Mood

- High-tech / low-life wartime dystopia.
- Grim determination and tactical urgency.
- Corporate authoritarianism vs. underground resistance.
- AI factions fighting ideological wars.
- Paranoia, rebellion, transhumanism, industrial militarism.

Relative to base `aesthetic.md`: same gravitas, more dread. The base aesthetic feels *noble*; the futuristic aesthetic feels *embattled*.

## What this mode does NOT change

- **Anachronism rule** (`aesthetic.md` §9) — still load-bearing. A WW2-era figure must be paired with a modern software artifact in-frame. Pure cyberpunk scenes without the WW2 grounding miss the brand.
- **Title / tagline grammar** (`aesthetic.md` §4, §8) — title bars, imperative-triplet taglines, ALL CAPS conventions all carry over.
- **Faction canon** (`aesthetic_faction_*.md`) — Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot keep their archetypes; this mode gives them denser environments, not new identities.
- **Aged-paper texture** — distressed propaganda finish survives every scene, no matter how much neon is present.
