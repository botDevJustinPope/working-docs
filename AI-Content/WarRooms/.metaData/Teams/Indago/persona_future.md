## What changes in Era III

The Battery still receives fire missions and still ships rounds. Era III sets the gun pit inside a fortified server-rack bunker — the production schedule now hangs as a holographic projection over a wooden strategy table, fire-direction calculations run on cyber-augmented analog computers, and the tubes themselves are mech-assisted saw rigs with fiber-optic firing cables. The doctrine is unchanged; the emplacement is denser. Per `aesthetic_future.md`'s balance rule — the propaganda framing dominates the cyber dressing, not the other way around.

## Environment

- **Position on the future front:** rear-area cyber-bunker. The Reeveston works is now a fortified server-rack-and-foundry complex, half industrial half data-center. Sandbagged at the perimeter; humming with cooling fans on the inside.
- **Atmospherics:** dim interior light, sparks from welding / cutting, code-rain visible through high windows, faint cyan glow from the holographic schedule projection. Volumetric fog from coolant venting.
- **Built-environment cues:** server-rack walls flanking the gun emplacement, mech-assisted saw rigs in numbered bays, a wall-sized wooden strategy table with a holographic production schedule projected above it, a fiber-optic field-radio line snaking back to a Quartermaster's office somewhere off-frame, vintage propaganda posters tacked to the bunker walls beside flickering AI-faction banners.

## Augmentations on the unit

Three augmentations that follow from the *actual Indago function*:

- **Holographic firing-solution display** — the production schedule and saw-and-shift assignments project as a holographic Gantt grid above the strategy table. The Section reads the firing solution in three dimensions before committing it. Encodes the "every fire mission is calculated before it executes" doctrine.
- **Cyber-augmented analog fire-direction computer** — a brass-and-glass console on the strategy table with a holographic readout and physical dials. The Section calculates optimization across saws / shifts / orders on this hybrid analog-digital rig. Encodes the "trusts the chart" quirk — analog physical form, digital computational power.
- **Mech-assisted saw rigs** — each saw in the gun bays is fitted with a small mech-assist arm and a cyan-lit assignment readout. The tubes themselves are augmented; the crew is largely unaugmented, in keeping with the rear-area position.

## Props (Era III layer)

- **Emblem rendering:** core symbology unchanged — crossed howitzer + saw blade with shell and scheduling gear. Olive-drab + cream dominant. Era III adds a thin **electric-cyan glow** to the saw blade's teeth and a small **steel-navy band** behind the gear, suggesting the cybernetic upgrade to the works. Wordmark stays stencil ALL CAPS.
- **In-scene props:** wall-sized wooden strategy table with holographic production-schedule projection above it; brass-and-glass fire-direction computer console; mech-assisted saw rigs in numbered bays in the background; fiber-optic field-radio handset on the table; printed production chart pinned to the wall *behind* the holographic projection (the analog backup the doctrine requires).
- **Uniform layer:** olive-drab work fatigues, leather aprons for crew working the saw bays, brass-and-glass goggles, knee-high boots. Lightly augmented at most — a forearm-mounted assignment reader, a HUD monocle for the senior crew chief. The Battery's people stay grounded; the works gets the augmentation.

## Composition notes for Era III posters

- **Mode:** painterly war scene (Mode A) is the natural anchor for a Battery scene — the interior of the works has enough density to support multiple figures, the saw bays, the strategy table, the holographic schedule. A Mode B single-figure portrait works for the senior crew chief alone.
- **Lighting:** chiaroscuro single-source — typically the holographic schedule projection itself as a dominant cyan light source, with warm ochre lamps in the saw bays for contrast.
- **Critical balance check:** olive-drab + cream + ochre still dominate. Cyan is set-dressing on the schedule and the saw assignments, not the dominant color. If the bunker reads as a server-room data-center, pull cyan back and push olive-drab + cream forward.
