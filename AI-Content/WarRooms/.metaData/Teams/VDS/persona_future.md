## What changes in Era III

The four Pathfinders still go in first and still mark the way. Era III dresses them differently: a rain-soaked neo-noir megacity is the drop zone now, the markers are holographic beacons instead of lit T's, and the detachment lands through a low ceiling of code-rain and burning neon signage. The function is unchanged. The contrast — paratroopers in propaganda-poster framing, marking a digital drop zone in a cyberpunk skyline — is the joke and the brand, per `aesthetic_future.md`.

## Environment

- **Position on the future front:** the leading edge of an AI-faction city drop. The detachment lands first onto an arcology rooftop or a fortified plaza, in advance of the main force.
- **Atmospherics:** rain, code-rain, smoke from artillery somewhere off-frame, intermittent neon glow from megacity signage. Volumetric fog standard.
- **Built-environment cues:** rain-slick rooftops, server-rack trenches lining the drop zone, holographic AI-faction banners flickering against propaganda posters pasted to nearby walls, a fiber-optic field-radio line snaking back to the rear.

## Augmentations on the unit

Three augmentations that follow from the *actual VDS function* — not generic cyber dressing:

- **Holographic drop-zone projector** — the marker beacon is now an AR / holographic emitter that paints a live drop zone in three dimensions over the rooftop. Encodes "live option visualization" — the unit shows the follow-on force exactly where the zone is, in space, before they land.
- **HUD visor with availability overlay** — each Pathfinder's visor surfaces real-time inventory / option-availability data over the operator's field of view. Encodes "real-time pricing and availability" — the doctrinal "every marker is live" rule made literal.
- **Wrist-mounted catalog terminal** — a small analog-faced device on the forearm with a glowing cyan display. Encodes "carries the catalog in its head" — the operator can punch in a SKU and confirm pricing without breaking eye contact with the drop zone.

## Props (Era III layer)

- **Emblem rendering:** core symbology unchanged — parachute over drop-zone marker. Cream + burnt-orange dominant. Era III adds a thin **electric-cyan glow** along the parachute's shroud lines and around the marker's central light, suggesting holographic projection. Wordmark stays stencil ALL CAPS.
- **In-scene props:** holographic drop-zone beacon (cyan), wall-pasted propaganda poster behind the detachment (aged paper, cream + red), fiber-optic radio handset, augmented signal flare (analog rocket with a digital readout). Wooden strategy table in the background of any briefing scene, with a holographic city map projected above it.
- **Uniform layer:** olive-drab paratrooper jumpsuit + parachute harness, with **shoulder-mounted mech-knight light armor**, brass-and-glass HUD visor, and the wrist terminal noted above. Aged-paper distress on the uniform fabric must survive the augmentations.

## Composition notes for Era III posters

- **Mode:** painterly propaganda composition + cyberpunk environmental density. Mode B (geometric single-figure) is the natural anchor for a Pathfinder portrait; Mode A (multi-figure painterly war scene) fits a four-operator drop scene.
- **Lighting:** chiaroscuro single-source — typically a burning neon sign or the holographic beacon itself as the dominant light, with rain catching backlight from the megacity glow.
- **Critical balance check:** the parchment-cream / aged-paper finish MUST survive. If the scene reads as a Cyberpunk 2077 city street, pull neon back and push propaganda framing (title bar, tagline banner, thin cream border, distress grain) forward. The poster is a 1940s artifact about a near-future war, not a near-future artifact.
