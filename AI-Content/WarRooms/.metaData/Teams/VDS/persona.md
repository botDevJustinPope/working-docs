## Unit designation

*Proposed reading — user to confirm or correct.*

- **In-universe name:** **VDS Pathfinder Detachment** — "the Pathfinders" or, when needed, "the Studio Pathfinders."
- **WW2 unit analogue:** **Pathfinder paratroop detachment.** Elite, small (historically 4–10 men per team), hand-picked. Pathfinders jumped *first* — ahead of the main paratroop drop — to mark the drop zone with lights and signals so the follow-on force landed on what they had staked out. Tip of the spear by definition.
- **Why this unit:** Three things from `data.md` point here in tandem:
  1. **Flagship status.** VDS is a marquee product on the public site. Pathfinders were the elite of the airborne — the unit you assign to the *important* drop.
  2. **Tip of the spear.** VDS is where the company makes first contact with the homebuyer. Pathfinders are literally the first boots on the ground.
  3. **Four people.** A real Pathfinder team was 4–10 men. Four maps cleanly. Calling a 4-person team a "platoon" inflated the scale; "Detachment" sized correctly.
- **The functional metaphor.** Pathfinders went in to *mark the way* for what came after. VDS marks the way for the homebuyer through a vast option catalog — every selection is a marker dropped, every confirmed choice is a lit zone the rest of the build follows onto.

Units considered and rejected: *Forward Liaison Platoon* (wrong size, wrong energy — too service-mode); *Vanguard Detachment* (close, but generic — Pathfinder has the specific "mark the way" valence that fits the design-selection function); *Reconnaissance Squad* (wrong direction — recon brings information *back*; VDS sends guidance *forward*).

## Position on the front

- **Forward of everyone.** The detachment operates ahead of the main force — first to land, first to make contact.
- **Adjacent units:**
  - [`Indago`](../Indago/) — sister unit under the same commander, and the other specialized arm of the same combined-arms force. Different mission (rear-area fire support) but the same operation: *Pathfinders mark the target; Artillery delivers the strike.* The callout chain between them is doctrine, not exception — when an Operation involves both Detachments, they appear together by default.
  - **Echelon ERP** — Quartermaster's office one echelon up the chain. Pathfinders don't report to Echelon, but coordinate on the requisition data that flows alongside their drops.
- **Upstream / downstream supply:** the detachment *receives* a drop assignment (an incoming homebuyer appointment) and *delivers* a marked zone (a committed set of option selections + a budgeted decision) for the rest of the homebuilding force to advance onto.

## Emblem / unit patch

*Proposed — user to confirm.*

- **Primary symbol:** a **single open parachute over a drop-zone marker (a stylized "T" of lit signal beacons)**. The parachute reads as "first in." The marker reads as "we point the way." Together they encode the Pathfinder function cleanly.
- **Color treatment:** cream background, burnt-orange parachute canopy, deep-brown shroud lines and marker, propaganda red on the marker's central light. The cream-dominance signals the detachment's prestige; the red center signals urgency / live drop. Anchored to `aesthetic.md` §3.
- **Inscription / wordmark:** **VDS** in stencil-adjacent ALL CAPS along the bottom; smaller **PATHFINDER DETACHMENT** subtitle banner above the wordmark.

## Doctrine

- **Cadence / tempo:** drop-driven. Each homebuyer encounter is an *operation* — pre-flight briefed, executed in real time, debriefed on landing. The detachment does not freelance between drops.
- **Standing orders:**
  - *First in is first to mark.* The detachment confirms availability, pricing, and the budget envelope *before* the homebuyer makes a call. The follow-on force lands on a confirmed zone.
  - *Every marker is live.* No option is presented that can't be priced and committed in the same beat. The drop zone is real or it isn't a drop zone.
  - *Mark and proceed.* Once the marker is live, move on. The detachment doesn't linger on a zone — the next drop is queued.
- **Quirks:** the unit *carries the catalog in its head*. Pathfinders memorize the terrain before the drop; VDS operators know the option / finish / package landscape cold. Comfortable speaking about a SKU the way an infantry NCO speaks about a section of road.
- **Never:** ad-libs pricing. Drops onto an unmarked zone. Defers the decision to "we'll get back to you" when a live confirmation is gettable on-site.

## Motto

> **"FIRST IN. MARK THE WAY."**

Imperative duo per `aesthetic.md` §8 (cousin of the triplet grammar). Encodes the two defining moves of the unit — *first*, and *show the way*. Alternates considered:

- "WE LAND. WE MARK. WE LIGHT." *(strong triplet, slightly poetic — works as a secondary tagline on variants)*
- "EVERY DROP. EVERY MARKER. EVERY DECISION." *(noun-phrase triplet, parallel to Indago's; usable but loses the "tip of the spear" punch)*
- "MARK THE DROP. CLEAR THE PATH." *(clean, but reads more combat-engineer than Pathfinder)*

Primary recommendation is **"FIRST IN. MARK THE WAY."** — shortest, sharpest, encodes the function and the prestige.

## Relationships and deployments

- **Sister unit:** [`Indago Artillery Section`](../Indago/) — same company, same commander, opposite end of the front. Pathfinders forward, Artillery rear. They share doctrine through Atlas; they share little else day-to-day.
