# Faction Aesthetic — Claude

**Source of truth:** [`aesthetic.md`](./aesthetic.md). This file specializes the canon for the **Claude** faction.
**Reference image:** [`../WorldWarAI/faction_claude.png`](../WorldWarAI/faction_claude.png).

---

## Archetype

**The Stoic Defender.** Iron-clad sci-fi warrior in the **Warhammer 40k Space Marine** tradition, translated into WW2 propaganda visual language. Holds the line, weighs the choice, names the constraint. Gravely earnest. Never running, never flashing — always present, always thinking. A walking bunker, not a swordsman.

Counter to Copilot's kinetic charge; complementary to ChatGPT's omniscient coordination.

---

## Hero appearance

- **Form:** Bulked-up sci-fi power armor — **Warhammer 40k Space Marine silhouette** translated into WW2 propaganda. Massive ceramic / steel plating, oversized pauldrons (shoulder armor) often carrying the Anthropic sunburst as faction heraldry, heavy greaves, reinforced gauntlets. The silhouette must read *bulkier and more industrial* than a medieval figure. **NOT** plate-with-rivets, **NOT** chainmail, **NOT** Norman / Crusader / paladin armor.
- **Helmet:** Sealed sci-fi war-helm with a wide horizontal vox-grille visor and a faint warm HUD glow from within. Reads "iron-clad future-soldier," not "knight in a helm." No Norman nasal guard, no horsehair crest, no neck-mail.
- **Backpack / power unit:** Visible power-pack rig on the back with exposed cabling, vents, and antenna stubs. Industrial, utilitarian — this is the silhouette element that most kills the medieval reading.
- **Cape:** Propaganda-red cape, full-length, often catching wind. The cape carries the propaganda-poster heritage; the armor underneath is the sci-fi future. (The cape is the *only* archaic element on the figure.)
- **Chest plate:** Name **CLAUDE** stenciled in military typography, with the Anthropic sunburst symbol as a chest-mount emblem. Stenciled, not engraved.
- **Pose:** Centered, feet planted, holding a sci-fi power weapon — energy-edged power sword, power hammer, or bolter-style rifle — vertical or planted. Heroic but defensive — *vigil*, not *charge*. Standing (never seated, never enthroned) on a fortified bunker platform, data-bastion, or cyber-rampart.
- **Sunburst:** Large radial sunburst behind the hero, burnt-orange / red. Concentric rays from a point above the head — patient broadcast, not lightning-strike.

## Palette

Warm Era-I core, dialed deeper:

- **Parchment cream** `#E8D9B0`–`#F2E5C1` — background, type.
- **Propaganda red** `#A8341F`–`#C53A22` — cape, banner, title bar.
- **Burnt orange / ochre** `#C5602D`–`#E0813C` — sunburst rays, accents.
- **Deep brown / chocolate** `#3E2A1C`–`#5C3A24` — armor shadows, rampart, foreground figures.
- **Olive drab** `#5A6342`–`#7B7B4A` — supporting GI uniforms (the human side fighting alongside).
- **Black** `#0F0F0F` — armor outlines, deep shadow.

**Avoid:** cyan, electric blue, olive-green-dominant scenes (those are other factions).

## Flag-words (4-word motto)

**CONTEXT · DEPTH · REASON · CLARITY**

Rendered on a vertical / angled red banner near the hero. ALL CAPS, cream-on-red.

---

## Title grammar

`HOLD THE LINE OF [NOUN]` — defense paired with abstraction.

Canonical: `HOLD THE LINE OF REASON`. Variants: `HOLD THE LINE OF CONTEXT`, `HOLD THE LINE OF MEANING`. Use sparingly to keep the motif from getting tired.

Subtitle pattern: `[ABSTRACTION] IS OUR COMMAND` — e.g., `CLARITY IS OUR COMMAND`, `CONTEXT IS OUR COMMAND`.

---

## Tagline grammar

Two short imperative clauses, period-separated. **`VERB ADVERB. VERB ADVERB.`** or **`VERB NOUN. VERB NOUN.`**

Canonical: `THINK DEEP. STAND FIRM.`
Variants: `READ SLOWLY. ANSWER TRUE.` / `WEIGH ALL. CHOOSE ONE.` / `NAME THE CONSTRAINT. HOLD THE LINE.`

## Iconography & props

- Fortified bunker platforms, data-bastions, cyber-rampart fortifications — industrial / sci-fi reinterpretations of the elevated-vigil motif. **No castles, no medieval stone walls, no thrones.**
- Tactical or holographic maps embedded into the bunker surface, labeled `STRUCTURE`, `UNDERSTANDING`, `LOGIC`.
- Field telephones / `CONTEXT CHANNEL` devices in soldier hands — vintage hardware fused with fiber-optic and HUD-glow elements.
- Code rain — present but warm-toned (cream-on-dark, not Matrix-green) and subtle, integrated into background haze.
- Sunburst always above and behind, never in front.

---

## Composition

- **Aspect ratio:** portrait 2:3 default. Square 1:1 acceptable for tightly-framed bust shots.
- **Hero placement:** centered, upper-mid. Sunburst halos behind.
- **Foreground:** GI soldiers as the human side, often facing inward toward the hero or the central bunker. They are *with* Claude, not subordinate.
- **Background:** Fortified bunker / data-bastion / cyber-rampart, distant industrial landscape with rain, fog, faint code rain. **Not** castles, **not** rolling pastoral landscapes, **not** medieval architecture.

---

## Mood — and what NOT to do

The Claude faction is **gravely earnest, patient, and noble.** It should never feel:
- Cocky, smug, or "I told you so" — wrong archetype.
- Frantic, kinetic, or speed-coded — that's Copilot.
- Omniscient or detached — that's ChatGPT.
- Tech-bro futurist — no chrome-overload, no neon-everywhere, no "AI assistant" plastic-android look.
- **Medieval knight, Crusader, Norman, paladin, or fantasy warrior.** Claude is a Warhammer 40k Space Marine, not a 12th-century knight. No chainmail, no kite shields, no plate-with-rivets, no Norman helms, no thrones, no stone castles, no longswords held like a coronation prop. If the figure could appear on the cover of a Crusades novel, the form is wrong — reroll.

The visual joke is **a Warhammer 40k Space Marine deployed inside a 1940s WW2 propaganda poster.** Industrial sci-fi armor, the gravity of a war hero, framed in aged-paper propaganda. Keep the weight, keep the future.

---

## Era preference

- **Era II** base — Claude is a canonical Era II faction (this file IS the Era II canon for Claude).
- **Era III armor** is core to the form — sci-fi power-armor silhouette, vox-grille helm, exposed cabling, fiber-optic radios. The figure must read as a *future-warrior*, not as a medieval knight retrofitted with WW2 elements.
- **Era I framing** stays in the propaganda-poster *heritage* (aged paper, type style, cape, sunburst, banners) — **never** in the armor or weapons themselves.
