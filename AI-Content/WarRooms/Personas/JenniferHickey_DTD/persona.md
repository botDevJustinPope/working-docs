#### Preceptor *(team's first recommendation)*

- **Meaning:** Senior military officer who tutors junior officers + academic teacher / instructor. Latin "praeceptor" = instructor.
- **Pronunciation:** pre-SEP-tor.
- **Real-world anchor:** Strongest blend of teacher + officer + technical-lead in the shortlist. Teacher root is direct (10 years at Cypress-Fairbanks ISD; MS in Curriculum and Instruction per [`data.md`](./data.md)). The military officer-tutor meaning maps cleanly onto her current PO seat — she instructs the dev team on what to build through user stories.
- **Historical reference:** Knights Templar Preceptors were both military commanders AND teachers of younger knights — the dual-role precedent maps to her arc.

#### Magister

- **Meaning:** Latin for "teacher / master." Magister Militum was a senior Roman military commander; the modern English "master" descends from it.
- **Pronunciation:** ma-JIS-ter.
- **Real-world anchor:** Classical resonance matches the cohort's existing Greek / Roman codenames (*Atlas*, *Archon*, *Chronos*). The "master" meaning captures both her teacher-mastery (decade in K-12 + MS in C&I per [`data.md`](./data.md)) and her current PO authority.
- **Risk:** More academic-feeling than military to a modern ear; QA-Lead facet is implied via mastery rather than literal.

**Next step:** await Jennifer's pick between the two. If neither lands, brainstorm reopens against the same anchor (teacher → QA → PO).
