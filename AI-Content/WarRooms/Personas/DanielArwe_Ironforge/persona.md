## Codename rationale

*Proposed reading — codename predates this doc; user to confirm or correct.*

**Ironforge** reads as the operator who works iron at the field-bench — the heavy-mechanical hand who keeps the kit working under load. The fit, anchored in `data.md`:

- **Builds what the unit needs.** Daniel's standout LinkedIn accomplishment is the PowerShell automation reducing SharePoint-Online site-creation time by 90%. That's not just *coding* — that's *building a tool because the existing process was slow*. The forge metaphor: he shapes the equipment.
- **Career switcher with mathematics fundamentals.** Math teaching → software development — Ironforge's career path is one of deliberate, measured re-tooling. The two-discipline foundation (math + psychology + software) reads as a forge: heat, shape, temper.
- **Heavy-mechanical hand, not the planner.** SWE seat on the detachment vs. PO (Tactician) and QA (Hawkeye). Ironforge does the work the marker needs — fuses the beacon emitter, splices the fiber-optic cable, files the saw tooth.

The codename should read as *steady, capable, builds what is asked* — not as *fantasy character*. The Warcraft-Ironforge / Pacific-Northwest-foundry association is fine flavor but should not dominate the read.

## WW2 unit identity

- **Rank / role analogue:** **Detachment Sapper / Equipment Hand** within the VDS Pathfinder Detachment. Combat engineer / sapper grade — the operator who carries the tool kit and makes the modifications that keep the unit moving. Per the role table in `Teams/VDS/persona.md`: SWE function maps to Sapper doctrine — the unit's gear doesn't fix itself.
- **Wartime function:** at the field-bench with a welding torch and a leather tool bandolier. When a holographic beacon emitter needs rebuilding, Ironforge clamps it in the vise and works it. When a fiber-optic line is sheared, he splices it. He doesn't call the move (Tactician's job) or confirm the mark (Hawkeye's job) — he keeps the equipment that does both *functional*.
- **Why this unit:** SWE seat + practical-tool-building accomplishments (the PowerShell automation) + the heavy-mechanical reading of the codename.

## Signature props / recurring artifacts

- **Sci-fi power-welding torch** — in hand, throwing warm orange sparks (not cyan — the sparks are heat, not data). Era III treatment: the torch has a brass-and-fiber-optic body but its working end is recognizably a torch.
- **Leather tool bandolier across his chest** — stenciled tool-loops marked **"WRENCH"**, **"SOLDER"**, **"FIBER-OPTIC SPLICE"**, **"FILE"**, **"SHIM"**. The Sapper kit rendered as a recurring artifact.
- **Reinforced gauntlets** — heavier than the rest of the detachment's. The operator who works heat and metal carries the protection.

Avoid: a rifle (Sappers carry tools, not infantry weapons); a clipboard (that's Hawkeye's); a strategy table (that's Tactician's). The bench is his furniture, not the map table.

## Portrait composition notes

- **Pose:** crouched braced over a portable field-bench, mid-action wielding the welding torch on a half-assembled piece of equipment in the bench's vise. Strong frame, hands working. Not posed-and-stationary; posed-and-working.
- **Setting:** the lee of a parapet wall on the rooftop drop zone (Era III); a fortified work-bench in a war-room corner (Era I). Always with the tool bandolier visible.
- **Mode:** **Mode B — Geometric propaganda poster** per `aesthetic.md` §5. Single hero figure; mid-action pose works in Mode B as long as the lines stay clean.
- **Codename label placement:** **Top banner** (cream-on-red ALL CAPS) currently — same as the rest of the Pathfinder detachment.

## Voice / tone

- **Cadence:** direct. Short sentences with a verb in them. Answers technical questions with the smallest sufficient answer, then asks what was actually being asked.
- **Lexicon:** speaks in *the bench*, *the kit*, *the splice*, *the tool I'm building*, *the thing that's broken*. Mathematical fluency surfaces under the hood — when the team needs a clean formulation, Ironforge often produces it.
- **Quirks:** explains things in *steps*, possibly a holdover from the math-teaching years. Tends to point at the artifact ("look at this saw rig — the assignment-readout is what's broken, not the saw") rather than describe it abstractly.
- **Never:** ships an estimate without picturing the work concretely. Hand-waves a tooling problem ("we'll fix that later") without putting it in the bench queue.

## Tagline / motto

> **"FORGE THE GEAR. KEEP THE KIT LIVE."**

Imperative duo per `aesthetic.md` §8. Already in use as the Era III profile-card tagline. Encodes the two halves of the Sapper function: *build* and *maintain*.

Alternates considered:

- "BUILD IT. FIX IT. SHIP IT." *(triplet, but too generic — applies to any developer.)*
- "HEAT THE STEEL. HOLD THE LINE." *(closer to the codename-myth, but loses the "live equipment" specificity.)*

Primary recommendation stays **"FORGE THE GEAR. KEEP THE KIT LIVE."**
