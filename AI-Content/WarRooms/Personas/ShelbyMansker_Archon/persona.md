## Codename rationale

**Archon** — ancient Greek *árchōn*, "ruler / chief magistrate." In classical Athens the archons were the senior officers of the polis who set policy and oversaw the courts; the word survives into English as a term for *senior, doctrine-setting authority by office*. The fit, anchored in `data.md`:

- **Software Architect by title, six-plus years.** The archon-as-magistrate read maps directly onto an architect's role: the senior authority who sets the pattern others build to. `data.md`: *"Application Programmer and Project Manager. Interested in leading small teams of programmers implementing Agile processes."* — and the architect title above that. The codename names what the title already says.
- **Institutional memory.** Shelby has been on the ePlanPartners → BuildOn lineage since November 2013 — ~12 yrs 7 mo, predating Eric Hickey (Atlas) by ~2 years on the Indago side. He is one of only two named personas on the active roster (alongside Rob Hobbs / Tactician) with the **pre-Eric-era ePlanPartners** vantage point. The archon is the one who *remembers what the doctrine used to say and why it was changed* — that institutional-memory role fits the codename's classical-magistrate framing.
- **Working manager, not pure officer.** `data.md` calls out the **Pitsco-era "working manager" pattern**: managed teams while still writing code. Archons in the classical sense were elected officials, not generals — they exercised authority by office, not by chain-of-command. That distinction matters here: Archon is a **doctrine-setting peer**, not a commander above the line. Atlas (Eric Hickey) commands the Company; Archon sets the architectural patterns the Company builds to.

**Heritage / role-posture framing:** the codename centers *authority by office* and *long-tenure institutional memory* — heritage of the codebase, role-posture of the senior craftsman-magistrate. No defector / refugee / displacement framing.

**The risk to watch:** Archon can read as *commander* if the portrait posture tips toward "officer above the line." The composition guidance below keeps him at a planning table, reviewing doctrine — not standing on a parapet directing a battle. The magistrate sits and rules on the pattern; the general stands and gives the order.

## WW2 unit identity

- **Rank / role analogue:** **General Staff Officer (Operations / G-3) at Theater Headquarters.** Field-grade staff officer — likely Major or Lieutenant Colonel — assigned to the operations branch of a multi-front theater HQ. Not the Theater Commander (that's Atlas at the Company level); not a detachment officer (those are Tactician, Atlas's company staff). Archon is the senior staff specialist who **sets operational doctrine across detachments** and **reviews the plan books before they go to the field**.
- **Wartime function:** at a large planning table inside a Theater HQ war room, reviewing a leather-bound doctrine manual — the consolidated playbook the rest of the theater is expected to operate by. Approves architectural changes (which sectors get which equipment, which pattern of advance is endorsed). Adjudicates when two detachments propose conflicting doctrines for the same problem.
- **Why this unit:** Software Architect = cross-product / cross-detachment pattern-setter → the staff-officer-at-Theater-HQ role is the WW2 analogue. Specifically G-3 (Operations) rather than G-1 (Personnel) or G-4 (Logistics): Archon sets *how the unit fights*, which is the architect's job applied to a software theater. The **12+ year ePlan → BuildOn continuous tenure** is what earns him the staff seat — he's the officer who's been at HQ longest and knows which patterns came from where.

## Tagline / motto

> **"SET THE DOCTRINE. STEWARD THE LINE."**

Imperative duo per `aesthetic.md` §8. Encodes the two halves of the archon-as-architect function: **set** the pattern (the doctrine-setting authority of the role) + **steward** what's already shipped (the institutional-memory across the 12-year ePlan → BuildOn lineage). Cream-on-dark ALL CAPS in the bottom banner.

Alternates considered:

- **"APPROVE THE PATTERN. PRESERVE THE LINEAGE."** — stronger archon-as-magistrate read; loses the "steward what's live" specificity that the working-manager Pitsco pattern earns.
- **"THE PATTERN HOLDS."** — single-line imperative, evocative; too cryptic without the doctrine-setting context.
- **"PRESERVE. APPROVE. ADVANCE."** — imperative triplet per the signature §8 grammar; rejected as too generic (could apply to any senior role).

Primary recommendation stays **"SET THE DOCTRINE. STEWARD THE LINE."**
