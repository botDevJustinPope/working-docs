This one **leans more futuristic and more cinematic than the rest of the set**: a darker, moodier sniper-hide scene rather than a bright parade poster. Keep the parchment-cream / aged-paper propaganda backbone and distressed print grain as the anchoring frame (palette: parchment cream, burnt orange, olive drab, propaganda red, deep brown), but allow electric cyan (#3FB3D8) and holographic blue (#1F5FA8–#3FB3D8) to play a larger role here as the light source in the dark. Painterly illustration with heavy cinematic chiaroscuro — stylized realism, not photoreal. **Balance check (relaxed for this poster):** a futuristic high-powered rifle and advanced tech ARE wanted here — do not down-res the weapon to WW2 gear. But the image should still read as part of the propaganda-poster set: keep the aged-paper finish, the title/subtitle/tagline banners, and the grain so it sits beside the other VDS posters rather than looking like a generic video-game render.
