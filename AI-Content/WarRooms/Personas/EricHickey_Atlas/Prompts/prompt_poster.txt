A WW2-era American propaganda **single-figure portrait** poster — Era I (classic 1940s base). Aged-paper texture and distressed print grain over a parchment-cream backbone (#E8D9B0–#F2E5C1), with burnt orange (#C5602D–#E0813C), olive drab (#5A6342–#7B7B4A), propaganda red (#A8341F–#C53A22), and deep brown (#3E2A1C–#5C3A24) carrying the composition. No modern flat-design pastels, no photoreal lighting, no gradient mesh. Painterly illustration with atmospheric warm lighting; faint sunburst rays optional behind the figure. The aged-paper, propaganda-poster finish must dominate.

**Hero figure (centered, three-quarter portrait):** **ATLAS** — a 1940s American **Colonel commanding a combined-arms task force**, brass-cap senior officer. Olive-drab service tunic with brass collar devices, leather Sam Browne belt across the chest, gloves tucked into the belt. Composed bearing — steady, unifying, *not* burdened. A man with twenty-five years of campaigns behind him. The **combined-arms commander** archetype: unifying authority over one operation built from two specialized units working in tandem — Pathfinders forward, Artillery behind — bound together by the callout chain.

**Pose / action:** standing behind a wooden **combined-arms situation map table in the foreground**, one hand bracing flat on the table edge, the other holding a **vintage field radio handset** at chest level — the literal link between Pathfinder marks and Artillery fire solutions, cord coiling down off the table to a field radio set on a side crate. A **leather-bound briefing book / field manual** rests on the table near his bracing hand, a small embossed **'DDD'** insignia visible on the cover as a regimental motto. Head turned slightly, eyes scanning the horizon out of frame. Composed; not posed mid-action — Atlas is *the steady one who closes the loop*.

**Background:** an interior war-room HQ. Behind/beside him, **two unit standards flank the figure** as the two specialized arms of one combined-arms force — a cream-and-red pennant marked **'DESIGN STUDIO'** on his left (the Pathfinder arm, forward, marks the way), an olive-and-ochre pennant marked **'INDAGO'** on his right (the Artillery arm, rear, delivers the strike). Both staffs lean slightly toward the center of the map table where Atlas stands. Behind him, a long horizontal **production-schedule chart pinned to the wall** — Gantt-style rows of saws and workstations across shifts, legible-as-a-schedule, the Indago Artillery Section's standing fire-plan. Beside it, a smaller **paper drop-zone schematic** with hand-drawn beacon positions — the VDS Pathfinder Detachment's operating area. Warm bunker lighting from a single overhead source.

**Combined-arms situation map (foreground, on the table):** a single hand-drawn topographic map showing the **forward drop zone** in the upper third and the **rear firing positions** in the lower third on the same sheet — hand-drawn coordinate lines, grease-pencil marks, and small pinned tokens connecting Pathfinder marker positions to Artillery fire-solutions. This is the doctrine made visible: one operation, two roles.

**Top:** narrower subtitle banner near the top reads **'BUILDON TECHNOLOGIES — DIRECTOR, SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT.'** in cream-on-olive ALL CAPS — role-line, not the codename.

**Bottom:** title plate at the base of the poster, bold cream-on-red ALL CAPS reading **'ATLAS'** — large, dominant codename ribbon. Below it, a slim tagline banner cream-on-dark, ALL CAPS imperative doublet: **'MARK THE TARGET. DELIVER THE STRIKE.'**
