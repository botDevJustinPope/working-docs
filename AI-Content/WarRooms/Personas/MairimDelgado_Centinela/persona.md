## Codename rationale

**Centinela** — Spanish for *"Sentinel."* The guard on post who watches for intrusion.

- **Pronunciation:** sen-tee-NAY-la.
- **Real-world anchor:** Mairim is a 10+ year QA professional with experience across drilling-sensor validation, financial credit-risk testing, and the Cuban national OS (GNU/Linux Nova). Spanish is her native language. The sentinel-on-post analogue maps QA = guard-on-the-line: she stands watch over what gets shipped, and bad code slipping past her is the equivalent of a sentry letting an intruder through the wire.
- **Why this codename, not another:** Strongest blend of QA-role + Spanish-language Cuban heritage in the shortlist. *Centinela* IS a QA word in Spanish; the WWII-era military analogue is literal (sentinel = guard duty). Differentiates cleanly from Walter Martinez (*Hawkeye* = sharp-eyed visual recon spotter) — *Hawkeye* watches the field from elevation; *Centinela* holds the line at the post.
- **Heritage celebration:** Spanish-language codename anchors her Cuban roots. The user explicitly framed this as celebration, not displacement (no defector / refugee narrative).
- **Grammatical note:** *Centinela* is grammatically masculine in Spanish ("el centinela") regardless of who holds the post. Not a problem — codenames don't strictly track gender.
