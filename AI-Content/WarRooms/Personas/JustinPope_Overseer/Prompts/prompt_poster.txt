A WW2-era American propaganda **single-figure portrait** poster — Era I (classic 1940s base). Aged-paper texture and distressed print grain over a parchment-cream backbone (#E8D9B0–#F2E5C1), with burnt orange (#C5602D–#E0813C), olive drab (#5A6342–#7B7B4A), propaganda red (#A8341F–#C53A22), and deep brown (#3E2A1C–#5C3A24) carrying the composition. No modern flat-design pastels, no photoreal lighting, no gradient mesh. Painterly illustration with atmospheric warm lighting; faint sunburst rays optional behind the figure. The aged-paper, propaganda-poster finish must dominate.

**Hero figure (centered, three-quarter portrait):** **OVERSEER** — a 1940s American senior NCO / Pathfinder, field-grade not officer-grade. Male soldier in olive-drab field uniform, khaki forage cap (no brass officer cap — he is on the parapet, not above the line), leather webbing belt, map case at his hip, sleeves rolled to the forearm. **Brass-and-glass field binoculars** hung at his chest on a worn leather strap — the heaviest optics in the detachment, encoding "watches multiple feeds at once." Steady, attentive expression — operator-reading-the-field, never managerial. The **senior-operator-on-the-parapet** archetype: one step removed from the immediate task, two steps closer to the broader picture than the junior operators.

**Pose / action:** standing slightly elevated on the lip of a sandbagged observation parapet, weight balanced, surveying the field across the war-room floor. **One hand rests flat on the parapet rail; the other is held mid-gesture above a leather-bound field ledger** propped open against the rail — he is reading a freshly-pinned overlay sheet. Eye line off-camera to the lower right, scanning the live picture, not posed for the viewer.

**Background:** an interior war-room observation balcony at dusk. Behind him: a tall planning wall with a topographic map, colored map pins, and overlapping field overlays. A vintage **field radio handset** sits within easy reach on the parapet beside him, cord coiled, hooked. A wooden briefing easel further back carries chalked operational notes. Warm bunker light from a low fixture catches the parchment of the documents. Distant other operators are silhouetted at the planning table below.

**Anachronism (per aesthetic.md §9):** the topmost overlay sheet pinned to the planning wall — and partially visible on his open field ledger — is a clearly-modern software artifact: a **'DEPLOY MANIFEST — VDS PATHFINDER / FEATURE FLAG REGISTRY'** sheet listing GUIDs, feature-flag toggles, and a `READY` stamp. The 1940s observation parapet framing a 2020s deploy manifest is the joke.

**Top:** title bar in bold cream-on-red ALL CAPS reading **'OVERSEER'** — large, dominant codename label, propaganda heritage unmistakable. Below the title, a narrower olive subtitle banner reads **'BUILDON TECHNOLOGIES — VDS PATHFINDER DETACHMENT.'**

**Bottom:** tagline banner, cream-on-dark, ALL CAPS imperative duo: **'EYES ON THE FIELD. HAND ON THE MARKER.'**

Thin cream frame border around the entire poster. Portrait 2:3 aspect ratio. Mode B (geometric single-figure propaganda) — single hero figure, flat color blocks, geometric simplification, one bold message. Aged-paper distress survives on the uniform fabric, the ledger paper, and the map overlay.
