## Codename rationale

*Proposed reading — codename predates this doc; user to confirm or correct.*

**Overseer** reads as the operator who *watches the whole picture*. Two strands point here:

- **Eyes-everywhere operational role.** Per `Teams/VDS/persona.md`: *"Overseer codename reads as the observer-of-the-whole-scene; fits the operator who works the zone and watches the broader field."*
- **The War Room sub-project itself.** Justin authored the persona schema, the aesthetic canon, the team write-ups, the Era III memo. The codename's literal meaning — *one who watches over* — fits the architect-of-the-universe role he plays in the working-docs.

The risk to watch: Overseer can read as *managerial* (which is wrong — Justin is a developer, not a manager). The codename should read as *senior operator on the parapet*, not *commander above the line*. Atlas is the commander; Overseer is the most-experienced field operator who *sees the whole field* without leading it.

## WW2 unit identity

- **Rank / role analogue:** **Detachment Operator / Senior Pathfinder** within the VDS Pathfinder Detachment. Higher experience than the rest of the detachment, but not in command. Reads the field the way a senior NCO would — one step removed from the immediate task, two steps closer to the broader picture than the junior operators.
- **Wartime function:** standing slightly elevated at a parapet, taking in the whole drop zone — Pathfinder beacons, the megacity beyond, the other operators working their marks. Hand near a radio handset, wrist-terminal showing live overlays. He doesn't run a single function (planning, verifying, building); he carries the operational picture across all of them.
- **Why this unit:** SWE seat + the sub-project authorship behavior (architect of the canon, observer across teams) + the codename's literal meaning.

## Signature props / recurring artifacts

- **Multi-overlay HUD visor** — the heaviest HUD in the detachment, surfacing multiple stacked callouts. Encodes "watches multiple feeds at once."
- **Forearm-mounted catalog terminal** — active with a holographic readout, more elaborate than the other operators' terminals.
- **Fiber-optic radio handset** — within easy reach at his belt or on the parapet beside him. The "calls the broader picture back to command" function.

Avoid: a Commander's brass cap (that's Atlas — Overseer is field-grade, not officer-grade); a strategy table (that's Tactician's).
