## Codename rationale

*Proposed reading — codename predates this doc; user to confirm or correct.*

**Anvil** reads as the *foundation* — the unmoving striking surface every other tool in the workshop works against. The fit, anchored in `data.md`:

- **Team Lead seat on Indago, five years continuous.** Per `data.md` (cross-referenced from `Teams/Indago/data.md`), Joseph is the **Team Lead** and the **longest-serving non-leadership member of the Indago dev team** — June 2021 to present. Every PO request, every junior-dev question, every architectural debate lands on the same surface. That surface is Anvil.
- **Migration ownership as repeated hammer-strikes.** Joseph **led the .NET Framework → .NET Core migration** on the Indago application, then **EF Framework → EF Core 3.1 → EF Core 7** after that. Each migration is a heavy strike against the codebase — and the team needs a foundation that doesn't move when struck. The Anvil takes the hammer; the codebase takes the shape.
- **Self-stated perseverance framing.** Joseph's LinkedIn summary opens with a John D. Rockefeller quote on perseverance — *the* explicit framing he uses for his own career arc (theater management ~10 years → late-college BBA in MIS → freelance → BuildOn). "Anvil" is the codename version of that framing: *built to take repeated impact without giving way.*
- **Career switcher with operational depth.** A decade running theaters (Carmike Managing Director, AMC General Manager) before the BBA-in-MIS and the BuildOn seat. The "Anvil" reading absorbs that: heavy use-prior, then re-purposed as foundation — not green steel, *tempered* steel.

The codename should read as *foundation, dependable striking surface, mentor to the workshop* — not *blunt force* or *weapon*. Anvils don't strike; they get struck, and the work happens *because* they don't move. **Heritage / role-posture framing only** — no defector / displacement narrative.

## WW2 unit identity

- **Rank / role analogue:** **Master Armorer at the Forward Field Ordnance Workshop** — Master Sergeant grade. The senior NCO who keeps the unit's primary weapon systems rebuildable. Owns the workshop bench and the apprentices working it.
- **Wartime function:** standing braced at the workshop anvil in a forward-base armorer's tent, schematic of a rifle action chalked on the anvil face, hammer in hand mid-strike against a glowing piece of steel held in the tongs. He rebuilds the rifle when it jams. He retools the bolt when the design changes. He trains the apprentices working the lighter benches around him.
- **Why this unit:** Team Lead seat on Indago (the *primary weapon system* of the Indago detachment is the Indago codebase itself) + repeated migration ownership (the heavy strikes that re-shape the application) + LinkedIn-attested mentorship of junior developers (the apprentices around the bench) + the codename's literal meaning.
- **Differentiation from Ironforge (Daniel Arwe, VDS Pathfinder Sapper):** *Ironforge* is the Sapper at the field bench, working the equipment forward — splices, fuses, makes the kit live. *Anvil* is the **foundation in the workshop**, the unmoving surface every architectural strike lands on. Sapper is forward-deployed and mobile; Anvil holds the workshop and is the workshop. They share the smith / metal-shop family but occupy different functional seats.

## Signature props / recurring artifacts

- **The forge anvil itself**, at his side — heavy, deep-brown iron with a chalked **'INDAGO'** schematic across its working face (an architectural diagram drawn in tradesman's chalk on the anvil's flat — anachronism cue: the schematic is a 2020s app architecture rendered in 1940s workshop chalk). The anvil propagates as Anvil's signature scene-furniture across pieces, the way Ironforge has his field-bench.
- **Long-handled blacksmith's hammer**, in hand or leaned against the anvil — the architectural-decision instrument. Heavy enough to be two-handed when needed; deliberate strike, not a swing-and-pray. Encodes "leads the migration" in iconography.
- **Leather migration ledger** at his belt or on a workshop side-table, embossed **'.NET FRAMEWORK → CORE'** on the cover — Joseph's specific migration trail rendered as a worn unit logbook. Recurring artifact; replaces the more-generic "clipboard" that other personas carry.

Avoid: a rifle (Anvil works *on* the rifle, doesn't carry one as identity); a strategy map table (that's the officer corps); a forward field bench (that's Ironforge's). The anvil is his furniture, the workshop is his ground.

## Portrait composition notes

- **Pose:** standing braced beside the anvil, weight evenly planted on both feet, hammer in his strong hand held at mid-haul (mid-strike or just-finished — *worked*, not posed). Other hand resting on the anvil's horn or holding the tongs. Eye line slightly off-camera — focused on the work, not on the viewer. Posed-and-working, the way Ironforge is, but heavier and more centered — Anvil is the foundation, so the figure should feel *planted*.
- **Setting:** interior of a forward-base armorer's workshop — wooden walls, a workbench behind him with apprentice-grade tools laid out, a stack of rifle stocks and bolt assemblies stacked on a shelf, a wall map of the Indago front pinned with the team's deployment markers. Warm forge-light from one side (a brazier or coal forge just off-frame) throwing chiaroscuro across the figure. In Era III the workshop becomes a server-rack-walled bunker with the same anvil, a glowing fiber-optic forge-source, and a holographic Indago schematic above the anvil face.
- **Mode:** **Mode B — Geometric propaganda poster** per `aesthetic.md` §5. Single hero figure, flat color blocks, geometric simplification — the anvil's silhouette is part of the composition's grounding line. Painterly background depth allowed in Era III.
- **Codename label placement:** **Top banner** (cream-on-red ALL CAPS) — matches the rest of the active roster (Centinela, Ironforge, Overseer convention).

## Tagline / motto

> **"HOLD THE STRIKE. SHAPE THE STEEL."**

Imperative duo per `aesthetic.md` §8. Encodes the two halves of the Anvil function: **hold** (the foundation under repeated impact — the Team Lead seat absorbing every architectural debate) and **shape** (the migration ownership — the codebase emerges in the form the strikes give it).

Alternates considered:

- *"TAKE THE STRIKE. KEEP THE SHAPE."* — closer to defensive; loses the *active shaping* role implied by migration leadership.
- *"FOUND THE LINE. FORGE THE BUILD."* — triplet-adjacent and on-theme, but "found" reads as ambiguous (foundation vs. discover). Pulled.

Primary recommendation stays **"HOLD THE STRIKE. SHAPE THE STEEL."**
