## Codename rationale

*Proposed reading — codename predates this doc; user to confirm or correct.*

**Tinker** reads as the operator who *adjusts the live machine without stopping it* — the implementer-below-the-lead whose specialty is small-mechanical work, precision fixes, and side-project hands-on building. Three strands from `data.md` point here:

- **Implementer-below-the-lead seat.** Per `Teams/Indago/data.md` Wade holds the **Software Developer** seat on the Indago Section, with Joseph Arellano (*Anvil*) as Section Chief / Team Lead above him. Anvil shapes the gun crew; Tinker is the crew hand who makes the adjustments under load. The codename anchors the *role posture*, not displacement — Tinker is the section's mechanical specialist, not a junior.
- **Career switcher who built his own path through tools.** Wade's career arc is pharmacy tech → CS degree (2017) → DigitalCrafts bootcamp (2018) → FDM consultant (2019–present). That trajectory is itself a tinkerer's path: keep re-working the kit until it fits the job. The codename honors the *deliberate, hands-on re-tooling* rather than treating the prior path as something to erase.
- **`github.com/welchsoft` portfolio.** Wade carries a public GitHub portfolio (linked from his LinkedIn). The `welchsoft` handle plus the **Python top-skill claim** that does *not* appear in his Indago stack (C# / .NET / EF Core / Aurelia / TypeScript per `Teams/Indago/data.md`) reads as **after-hours tinkerings** — side projects in a different language than the day job. That's the literal *tinker* signature: builds things on the workbench when the foundry is quiet. (*Inferred — the welchsoft contents are not yet inventoried; see `data.md` open questions.*)

The codename should read as *steady, hands-on, precision-mechanical* — not as *amateur* or *hobbyist*. A tinker in the WW2 sense is a specialist tradesman: the operator the section calls when the gun won't seat, not the kid taking apart a radio for fun.

## WW2 unit identity

- **Rank / role analogue:** **Gun Crew / Tube Mechanic** within the Indago Artillery Section. Per `Teams/Indago/persona.md` composition table — locked role for the Software Developer seat on the Section.
- **Wartime function:** at the base of the gun emplacement, working on a live tube between fire missions. When a saw's feed-rate drifts out of tolerance, Tinker is the crew hand who crouches beside the rig with a wrench and a precision micrometer and brings it back into spec. He doesn't compute the firing solution (that's *DTD*, Fire Direction Officer) or run the gun pit (that's *Anvil*, Section Chief) — he keeps the *tube itself* firing.
- **Why this unit:** SWE seat on Indago = Tube Mechanic per the Section's role table. The mapping is direct: the implementer who works on live equipment, takes assignments from the Section Chief, and is measured by whether the gun stays hot through the shift. The codename *Tinker* fits this seat better than any alternative — small-mechanical precision is exactly the Gun Crew Tube Mechanic's job description.
