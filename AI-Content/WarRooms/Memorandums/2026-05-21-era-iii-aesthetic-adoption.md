## Background

The War Room visual program has, until now, lived almost entirely in **Era I** — classic 1940s American wartime propaganda, parchment cream and burnt orange, GIs around a strategy table arguing about a feature flag. **Era II** introduced the AI factions (Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot) for cross-cutting AI themes.

We are formally turning on **Era III — "Near-future cyber-WW2."** Same propaganda heritage, but dressed for a rain-soaked neo-noir megacity where the front is the network and the trench is a server rack. WW2-era paratroopers fused with cybernetic augmentations — HUD visors, mech-knight shoulder armor, wrist-mounted catalog terminals — operating under volumetric fog, code-rain, and holographic battle maps.

The canon lives at **`AI-Content/WarRooms/.metaData/aesthetic_future.md`**. Era III is intentionally restrained — use it for milestone moments and ambitious work, not as a default. The aged-paper, distressed-propaganda backbone must survive every Era III scene; if a poster reads as Cyberpunk-2077 fan art instead of WW2 propaganda with cyberpunk elements, the balance has tipped wrong.

A small but important rule we just locked in: **AI factions (Claude / Copilot / ChatGPT) are additive, not required.** A standard PBI Operation poster — bug fix, schema change, feature flag, copy revision — is the detachment alone in its environment. Factions appear *only* when the work item is itself about AI collaboration or cross-cutting AI themes.

## About the Detachment

**VDS** — internally, the **VEO® Design Studio team** — owns BuildOn Technologies' flagship customer-facing product. VEO® Design Studio is the software the homebuyer experiences at the design center: real-time option and finish selection, live pricing, budget visualization, pre-appointment design preparation. We sit at the front of the homebuyer journey — first contact, first commitment, the first lit zone the rest of the build lands on.

In the War Room, VDS is the **Pathfinder Detachment**.

> Pathfinders were the elite paratroopers who jumped *first* — ahead of the main drop — to mark the zone with lights and signals so the follow-on force landed on what they had staked out. Four to ten operators per team. Tip of the spear by definition.

The metaphor maps cleanly. We go in first. We mark the way. Every confirmed selection is a beacon dropped. The rest of the homebuilding force advances onto the zone we've lit. **Four operators**, sized to a real Pathfinder team, under one company commander.

**Motto:** **FIRST IN. MARK THE WAY.**

**Doctrine highlights:**
- *First in is first to mark.* The detachment confirms availability, pricing, and the budget envelope *before* the homebuyer commits.
- *Every marker is live.* No option is presented that cannot be priced and committed in the same beat.
- *Mark and proceed.* Once a marker is live, the detachment doesn't linger — the next drop is queued.

Full unit canon: `AI-Content/WarRooms/.metaData/Teams/VDS/persona.md` (Era I) and `persona_future.md` (Era III specialization).
